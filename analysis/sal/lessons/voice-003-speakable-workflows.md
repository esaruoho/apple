# Voice 003: Speakable Workflows

Track: Accessibility And Voice
Level: 3
Category: Accessibility And Voice History

## Source

- Site: `macosxautomation`
- Page: `sources/sal/macosxautomation.com/mirror/mavericks/speakableworkflows/index.html`

## Goal

Connect pre-Shortcuts voice workflows to Sal's broader automation philosophy.

## What This Lesson Allows

- Show the lineage from speakable workflows to Siri and Shortcuts-era ideas
- Explain that voice automation existed inside the Mac automation tradition long before recent tools
- Preserve the historical continuity of Sal's accessibility teaching

## Repo Pairing

- Workflow: `scripts/workflows/homepod/homepod-lights-off.applescript`

Why this pairing:
It captures the feel of a speakable automation outcome: a short command surface mapped to an immediate real-world effect.

## Suggested Teaching Flow

1. Read the speakable workflows page.
2. Discuss what made a workflow "speakable" in older macOS systems.
3. Run the lights-off workflow.
4. Compare the old model with Shortcut-backed AppleScript bridges.

## Practice

- Compare `scripts/workflows/homepod/homepod-lights-off.applescript` with `scripts/workflows/homepod/homepod-lights-on.applescript`.
- Describe a modern speakable workflow you would want in Home, Finder, or Notes.

## Modern Equivalent

- Type: `shortcut-bridge`
- Target: `scripts/workflows/homepod/homepod-lights-off.applescript`

## Outcome

The learner sees voice control as a continuous thread in Mac automation history rather than a disconnected modern feature.
