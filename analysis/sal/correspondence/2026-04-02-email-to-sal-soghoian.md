# Email to Sal Soghoian

Subject: Preserving your automation sites and asking about 6 remaining archive gaps

Hi Sal,

My name is Esa Ruoho. I maintain the open-source Apple automation repository at:
https://github.com/esaruoho/apple

I wanted to reach out because a major part of the repo has become a preservation and teaching effort around your automation work.

The short version is this: Apple did not preserve this material institutionally, and I’ve been trying to preserve it in usable form.

What we’ve done so far:
- mirrored and indexed major parts of macosxautomation.com, iworkautomation.com, photosautomation.com, configautomation.com, and dictationcommands.com
- extracted 597 inline AppleScript examples from the preserved sites
- built 38 lesson modules across 6 curriculum tracks from the preserved source material
- recovered 232 download/media targets so far
- kept large media local for later transcription instead of pushing them into git history
- built machine-readable indexes and status tooling so the archive stays honest about what is present and what is still missing

What this repo is trying to do now:
- preserve the original source corpus
- preserve the runnable scripts, installers, bundles, and demo media
- turn the preserved material into a working Apple automation library and curriculum
- continue the automation worldview you advocated: local, readable, user-controlled, workflow-oriented automation

We are now down to 6 known missing artifacts:
- iworkautomation.com/numbers/PresidentsSQLiteDB.zip
- macosxautomation.com/405/us/media/apple/applescript/2008/aperturepdfworkflows.zip
- macosxautomation.com/applescript/apps/Script_Geek.zip
- macosxautomation.com/applescript/apps/Script_Geek_old.zip
- photosautomation.com/installer.zip
- photosautomation.com/Photos-to-Keynote.mp4

The biggest conceptual gap is Dictation Commands. We have partial pages and some demo coverage, but we do not yet have enough to reconstruct a full runnable Dictation Commands app/package corpus.

So I wanted to ask directly:

1. Do you happen to have local copies of any of the 6 missing files above?
2. Do you have surviving installer/app/package material for Dictation Commands?
3. Are there offline backups, conference kits, CMD-D materials, demo bundles, or old archive folders that might contain the missing payload layer?
4. Is there any preservation or attribution preference you would like us to follow as this work continues?

If helpful, I can also send a compact status summary of what has already been preserved and how it is organized in the repo.

Thank you for the decades of work you put into making automation understandable and humane. A lot of us are still learning from it.

Best,
Esa Ruoho
https://github.com/esaruoho/apple
