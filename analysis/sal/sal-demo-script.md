# How to use this script

You: have **Hey Sal** wired as a Vocal Shortcut (System Settings → Accessibility → Speech → Vocal Shortcuts → Hey Sal).

For each step below:

1. Say **"Hey Sal"**
2. After the dictation prompt opens, say the **bold phrase**
3. Watch the action happen
4. When you're ready for the next step, move down

The Mac is doing the work. Hey Sal is the recognition layer. The user Shortcuts are the actions. This is exactly Sal's 2016 architecture, rebuilt on 2026 macOS.

---

## Stage 1 — Open Photos and select

> **"Switch to Photos"** — Photos.app comes to the foreground.

> **"Select all photos"** — All photos in the current view are selected (Cmd-A).

---

## Stage 2 — Build a presentation

> **"Make a new presentation"** — Keynote opens with a blank new presentation.

> **"Add a blank slide"** — A new blank slide appears in the front Keynote document.

---

## Stage 5 — Apply transitions

> **"Apply magic move"** — Current slide gets a Magic Move transition.

> **"Apply dissolve"** — Current slide gets a Dissolve transition.

> **"No transition"** — Current slide's transition is removed.

---

## Stage 8 — Manipulate slides

> **"Duplicate current slide"** — Current slide is duplicated.

> **"Next slide"** — Move to the next slide.

> **"Previous slide"** — Move to the previous slide.

> **"Go to first slide"** — Jump to slide 1.

> **"Go to last slide"** — Jump to the last slide.

---

## Bonus — Clipboard ↔ Slide

(First, copy some text or a URL to your clipboard. Then:)

> **"Insert clipboard content to title on slide"** — Whatever's on your clipboard becomes the title of the current Keynote slide.

> **"QR this"** — Whatever's on your clipboard becomes a QR code image inserted into the current slide.

---

## Stage 7 — Save

> **"Save"** — Front document saves.
