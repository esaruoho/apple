# Steve Jobs Stories Sal Soghoian Told

Four Jobs anecdotes Sal told (or that frame his career around Jobs) in the WIRED 2018 profile by Jordan McMahon.

## 1. The 1997 Confrontation — "I'm Sal Soghoian, and you're wrong"

Just after Jobs returned as interim CEO (July 1997, post-Amelio ouster), stock was plummeting and Jobs was on a slash-and-burn tour through under-performing departments. In the room he said:

> "It used to be easy when we were 100 times better than Windows. But now that we're not, you don't know what to do." — Steve Jobs

Sal — Apple's product manager of automation — was the **first person in the room to push back**:

> Sal: "No, you're wrong."
> Jobs: "And you are?"
> Sal: "I'm Sal Soghoian, and you're wrong. My technology is better than Windows."

Sal's own framing of the moment:

> "I sort of saw it as 'I might be this dog on my square yard of dirt, but I know every bit of that square yard and you're stepping on my yard. I'm gonna bite your leg.'"

Jobs was gauging who in the room was passionate enough to fight for their work — those were the people he wanted to keep. Sal passed the test and was hired into the product manager of automation role shortly after.

## 2. The Hallway Stakeout (2004) — Pitching Automator

Sal had built the original Automator prototype but couldn't get anyone at Apple to bite. With WWDC approaching, he tried to get into the office where Jobs's crew was meeting and was denied entry.

**He camped in the hallway from 10am to 5pm**, vowing not to leave until he got a minute with Jobs.

As Jobs left the office, he saw Sal lingering and asked what he wanted to talk about. Game face on, Sal pitched:

> "Automation, but for the rest of us." — Sal Soghoian

They scurried into a conference room for a private demo. The demo: take a Safari webpage of family-photo thumbnails, find the full-sized versions, load them into iPhoto, and burn to DVD — all from a chain of clicks. Jobs's reaction:

> "Stop! I want robots for icons." — Steve Jobs

Automator shipped on **April 29, 2005** — robot icon and all.

## 3. "Saul, Whom You All Know" — WWDC 2004

Before Sal went on stage at WWDC 2004, he and Jobs rehearsed repeatedly. Jobs never got his name right:

> "He'd tell me 'No, Saul, you want to do this.' He never quite got my name right." — Sal

Jobs introduced him to the WWDC crowd:

> "I'd like to invite Saul up on the stage, whom you all know." — Steve Jobs

The next day, Sal found a new name tag on his office door:

> **"Saul, whom you all know."**

## 4. The Dismissal (October 2016) — Sal Frames It Through Jobs's Absence

Not a Jobs anecdote directly, but Sal frames his 2016 firing in Jobs terms. After a nearly 20-year stint, Apple told him his position didn't exist anymore — no warning, no early signs.

> It had been thirteen years since Automator debuted on the Mac, and Soghoian's biggest champion at Apple, Steve Jobs, was gone.

Sal's response:

> "If anything, it's a change in something you've known for a long time. But I still have more work to do." — Sal Soghoian

## Pattern

The Jobs–Sal arc has three acts: **confrontation** (1997, earned the role), **hallway pitch** (2004, earned Automator), **absence** (2016, lost the role). Sal's career inside Apple is bracketed by direct Jobs encounters — and ends with Jobs gone.
